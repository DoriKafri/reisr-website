Common questions

Answers before you need to ask

Can I buy property in Israel from abroad?▼
Yes. There are no restrictions on foreign nationals purchasing property in Israel. The process can be managed remotely with the right team — a buyer's advocate, a local lawyer, and a power of attorney where needed. Many of our clients complete most of the process without traveling, and visit only for final viewings or signing.
Do I need to visit Israel to buy?▼
Not necessarily, though we recommend at least one visit for final viewings if possible. We conduct preliminary research, video walkthroughs, and neighborhood tours remotely. For clients who cannot travel, we can coordinate the full process using power of attorney and our local partner network.
How do your fees work?▼
We charge a transparent, pre-agreed buyer advisory fee. This is discussed upfront during your discovery call and formalized before any work begins. We do not receive commissions from sellers or developers — our incentive is aligned entirely with yours. Full fee details are provided in our engagement letter.
What's the difference between buying resale and new-build?▼
New-build (off-plan) means purchasing from a developer before or during construction. You may wait 2–4 years for delivery, but get a brand-new home with a warranty. Resale means buying an existing property from a private owner — you can move in quickly and see exactly what you're getting. Each has different legal, financial, and practical considerations. We help with both.
What other professionals will I need?▼
At minimum, you'll need an Israeli real estate lawyer. Depending on your situation, you may also need a mortgage broker, tax advisor (especially for international buyers), building inspector, and currency exchange service. We coordinate with vetted professionals in each category and can introduce you to trusted partners.
Do you only work with Jewish buyers or olim?▼
No. We work with any international buyer purchasing property in Israel. Our expertise in aliyah-related housing planning means we understand the specific needs and timelines of olim, but our services are available to anyone buying in Israel regardless of background or residency status.

Who Tel Aviv is right for

Tel Aviv suits buyers who want walkable urban living, proximity to tech and business hubs, vibrant nightlife and culture, and beach access. It's Israel's most expensive market, but it's also the most liquid — properties here hold value and resell relatively quickly. Best for: young professionals, dual-income families, second-home buyers who want energy, and long-term investors betting on continued demand.

Neighborhoods to know

Each neighborhood in Tel Aviv has its own character, price point, and buyer profile.

Neve Tzedek

Boutique, historic, premium

Tel Aviv's oldest neighborhood, now its most prestigious. Restored Ottoman-era buildings, designer boutiques, and top restaurants. Best for: design-minded buyers, premium second homes.

Florentin

Creative, edgy, gentrifying

Street art, independent cafes, and a younger demographic. More affordable than central neighborhoods but rapidly appreciating. Best for: investors, young buyers, creative professionals.

Lev Ha'ir (City Center)

Central, walkable, diverse

The beating heart of Tel Aviv — Rothschild Boulevard, Dizengoff, Bauhaus architecture. Mix of old and new buildings. Best for: buyers who want to be in the middle of everything.

Old North

Residential, family-friendly, established

Quieter than the center but still walkable to the beach and park. Popular with families and expats. Good school proximity. Best for: families, long-term residents, lifestyle buyers.

Sarona / Kaplan

Modern, towers, business district

New high-rise towers near the Azrieli center and Sarona market. Modern amenities, concierge buildings. Best for: professionals, investors in premium rental, buyers wanting new construction.

Jaffa

Historic, artistic, mixed

Ancient port city merged with Tel Aviv. Galleries, flea markets, stunning sea views. A mix of restored old and bold new projects. Best for: character seekers, art lovers, unique property hunters.

Practical buying notes

Purchase tax for non-residents starts at 8% of property value. New developments typically price at a premium but may offer payment plans spread over 2-3 years of construction. Resale transactions move faster but require thorough legal and structural due diligence, especially in older Bauhaus-era buildings. Many buildings in central Tel Aviv are undergoing TAMA 38 seismic reinforcement, which can significantly affect value.

Who Jerusalem is right for

Jerusalem attracts heritage-connected buyers, religious families, and investors targeting the diaspora rental market. It's a city of deep community ties, and real estate here often has emotional significance beyond pure financial return. Jerusalem leads Israel in foreign purchases — 44% of all non-resident apartment acquisitions happen here.

Neighborhoods to know

Rehavia

Prestigious, leafy, central

Jerusalem's most sought-after residential area. Tree-lined streets, proximity to the Old City, top schools. Consistently high demand from both locals and diaspora buyers.

Talbiyeh

Elegant, quiet, diplomatic

Historic mansions and modern apartments near the Israel Museum. Popular with embassy staff and affluent families. Ultra-luxury segment growing rapidly.

German Colony (Moshava)

Trendy, dining, family-friendly

Emek Refaim Street is Jerusalem's lifestyle hub. Cafes, shops, and a strong Anglo community. Good mix of historic charm and modern convenience.

Arnona / Talpiot

Accessible, growing, value

More affordable entry point with excellent connectivity. New developments are transforming these areas. Best for: first-time buyers and investors seeking yield.

Practical buying notes

Jerusalem has seen a surge in organized diaspora investment groups buying entire buildings. Foreign resident purchase tax is 8-10%. The city offers strong rental demand from students, young professionals, and short-term visitors. Many newer projects in areas like Arnona offer modern specifications at lower price points than central neighborhoods.

Who Netanya is right for

Netanya is Israel's most popular destination for Anglo and French-speaking buyers seeking beachfront living at accessible prices. The city has a large, established English-speaking community and a relaxed coastal vibe that appeals to retirees, families, and investors looking for better yields than Tel Aviv offers.

Neighborhoods to know

South Beach (Ir Yamim)

Premium beachfront, modern

Netanya's most upscale area with new high-rise towers and direct beach access. Strong demand from international buyers. Best for: premium coastal living, second homes.

City Center

Walkable, affordable, renewing

Close to the cliff promenade and main shopping areas. Older buildings offer entry-level prices with urban renewal potential. Best for: investors, first-time buyers.

Poleg

Family-friendly, new builds

Southern Netanya's growth hub with new schools, shopping centers, and residential developments. Best for: families making aliyah who want modern infrastructure.

Practical buying notes

Netanya offers significantly lower entry prices than Tel Aviv (30 minutes by train). Many new beachfront developments target international buyers with payment plans and English-language sales. The city's ongoing cliff stabilization and promenade projects are boosting coastal property values. Strong Anglo community means English-speaking services are readily available.

Who Ra'anana is right for

Ra'anana is Israel's premier suburban family destination for English-speaking immigrants. Top-rated schools, beautiful parks, a thriving Anglo community, and easy access to the Tel Aviv metro area make it the default choice for many families making aliyah from North America and the UK.

Neighborhoods to know

North Ra'anana

Premium, villas, established

Spacious homes, tree-lined streets, and proximity to the city park. The most desirable area for families with larger budgets. Best for: families wanting space and community.

New Ra'anana (South)

Modern, towers, walkable

Newer high-rise developments with modern amenities, close to the train station and commercial centers. Best for: young families and professionals wanting convenience.

City Center

Lively, restaurants, compact

Ahuza Street is the main artery — cafes, shops, and community life. Older buildings at more accessible prices. Best for: buyers wanting walkable urban convenience.

Practical buying notes

Ra'anana commands a premium for its school system and community infrastructure. Properties here tend to hold value exceptionally well. The city is connected to Tel Aviv by train (20 min) and Highway 531. Demand from olim consistently outpaces supply, particularly for 4-5 bedroom family apartments and cottages.

Who Haifa is right for

Haifa offers Israel's best combination of yield and affordability. It's a university city with diverse culture, mountain-sea views, and a genuine urban renewal story. Best for: yield-focused investors, buyers who want value, students and academics, and anyone drawn to Haifa's unique character — a city that doesn't try to be Tel Aviv.

Neighborhoods to know

Carmel Center

Scenic, established, panoramic

Haifa's premium hilltop area with stunning bay views. Restaurants, cultural venues, and Baha'i Gardens proximity. Best for: lifestyle buyers who want Haifa's best address.

German Colony (HaMoshava)

Renovated, trendy, growing

Ben Gurion Boulevard is Haifa's cafe and restaurant strip. Restored Templar-era buildings alongside new projects. Strong appreciation trajectory.

Hadar

Affordable, student-heavy, reviving

Haifa's most affordable central area. Strong rental demand from Technion and university students. Urban renewal is accelerating. Best for: yield-focused investors.

Practical buying notes

Haifa offers the highest rental yields of any major Israeli city. The Technion and University of Haifa create consistent student rental demand. New infrastructure projects including the cable car and port redevelopment are boosting the city's appeal. Entry prices are 60-70% below Tel Aviv for comparable quality.

Who Beit Shemesh is right for

Beit Shemesh has become Israel's fastest-growing Anglo religious community. The city's newer neighborhoods — Ramat Beit Shemesh Aleph, Bet, Gimmel — are designed communities with synagogues, schools, and infrastructure built for religious family life. It's one of the few places in Israel where you can buy new construction at reasonable prices with a ready-made community waiting.

Neighborhoods to know

Ramat Beit Shemesh Aleph

Established Anglo, religious

The original Anglo neighborhood with the most established community. English widely spoken, familiar institutions, and proven property values.

RBS Gimmel / Daled

New builds, modern, growing

Newest development areas with modern apartments at the most competitive prices. Growing communities but still developing infrastructure.

Old Beit Shemesh

Affordable, diverse, urban

The original city center. More diverse and affordable, with good transit connections. Urban renewal projects are gradually transforming the area.

Practical buying notes

Beit Shemesh offers excellent value compared to Jerusalem (20 min by train). New construction is plentiful with multiple developers competing. The city's rapid growth means infrastructure sometimes lags behind development — verify school capacity and transit plans before committing to newer neighborhoods.

Who Ashdod is right for

Ashdod is Israel's sixth-largest city and a growing coastal destination, particularly popular with French-speaking immigrants. It offers beachfront living at significantly lower prices than Netanya or Tel Aviv, with modern city infrastructure and improving cultural amenities. Best for: affordable coastal living, French-speaking community ties, and yield-focused investment.

Neighborhoods to know

City (Yud-Zayin)

New, beachfront, premium

Ashdod's newest waterfront neighborhood with modern towers and sea views. The city's premium residential area with new commercial centers.

Marina Area

Recreational, modern

Near the port and marina with restaurants and entertainment. Growing appeal among younger buyers and investors. Strong rental potential.

Older Neighborhoods (Aleph-Daled)

Affordable, renewal potential

Entry-level pricing with urban renewal upside. Some buildings are undergoing TAMA 38 reinforcement. Best for: budget investors seeking appreciation.

Practical buying notes

Ashdod is connected to Tel Aviv by train (35 min) and has Israel's largest port driving economic growth. The French-speaking community provides a strong social network for francophone buyers. New beachfront developments offer modern living at 40-50% below comparable Netanya or Tel Aviv prices.

Who Ramat Gan is right for

Ramat Gan is Tel Aviv's savvy neighbor — offering comparable urban energy at significantly lower prices. Home to the Diamond Exchange, Bar-Ilan University, the national stadium, and Safari park, it has its own strong identity while being essentially integrated with greater Tel Aviv. Best for: buyers who want Tel Aviv-area living without Tel Aviv prices, investors betting on continued urban renewal.

Neighborhoods to know

Diamond Exchange Area

Towers, business, modern

Israel's tallest buildings and a major business hub. Luxury high-rises with views of Tel Aviv. Strong demand from professionals and executives.

City Center

Renewing, accessible, diverse

Older buildings with active urban renewal (pinui-binui) projects. Walkable to parks and commercial areas. Significant appreciation potential as projects complete.

Kiryat Krinitzi

Residential, quiet, family

Peaceful residential area near the Safari park. Lower density, more green space. Good value for families wanting a quieter setting within greater Tel Aviv.

Practical buying notes

Ramat Gan is undergoing massive urban renewal with dozens of pinui-binui projects replacing old buildings with modern towers. This creates both opportunity (buying pre-renewal for appreciation) and risk (construction disruption, timeline uncertainty). The Diamond Exchange area is Israel's emerging premium high-rise district with some of the country's tallest residential towers.
